# Show Your Work — Kleon's Sharing Framework

A Claude skill distilled from *Show Your Work!* by Austin Kleon.

## What It Does

This skill helps creators, makers, and knowledge workers build an audience by sharing their process publicly — not polished finished work, but the daily reality of how things get made.

It applies Kleon's 10-principle framework to help you answer:
- What should I share about my work?
- How do I document my process without it feeling like self-promotion?
- What's the difference between sharing and human spam?
- How do I build a sustainable daily practice of sharing?
- How do I turn daily updates into durable content that compounds over time?

## Core Concepts Applied

**Scenius** — Creativity as collaborative ecology, not lone genius. How to participate in your creative community rather than broadcasting at it.

**Amateur advantage** — Why sharing while you're still learning is more powerful than waiting until you're an expert.

**Daily dispatch** — The practice of sharing one small piece of your process every day.

**Stock and flow** — Balancing ephemeral daily updates (flow) with durable long-form content (stock) that remains findable years later.

**"So What?" test** — Evaluating whether content adds genuine value before posting it.

## When to Use This Skill

- You're a creator (writer, designer, developer, maker, artist) who wants to build an audience but feels uncomfortable with self-promotion
- You have good work but no one knows you exist
- You're unsure what's worth sharing vs. what's too raw or too boring
- You want to build a daily sharing practice that's sustainable
- You're trying to figure out how to use social media in a way that doesn't feel hollow
